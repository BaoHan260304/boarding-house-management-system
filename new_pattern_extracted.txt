II. Requirement Specification
II.1 Problem description
The Boarding House Management System is a software solution designed to support landlords and staff in managing boarding houses, tenants, rooms, rental contracts, and related services more effectively. Traditional boarding house management methods often rely on manual paperwork, phone calls, and spreadsheets, which can lead to data inconsistency, delayed communication, and difficulties in tracking rental information and payments.
This system aims to digitalize and automate the management process by providing centralized features for room management, tenant management, contract handling, payment tracking, maintenance requests, and report generation. The system also improves communication between landlords, staff, and tenants through notifications and service updates.
The context diagram illustrates the interaction between the Boarding House Management System and external entities including Landlord, Staff, Tenant, and Bank/E-Wallet services. Landlords can manage room information, rental requests, occupancy reports, and revenue reports. Staff members are responsible for updating tenant information, billing information, room status, and payment records. Tenants can register rooms, make payments, submit maintenance requests, search for available rooms, and receive utility bills and notifications. In addition, the system integrates with Bank/E-Wallet services to process payment requests and confirmations.
By implementing this system, boarding house operations become more organized, transparent, accurate, and convenient for all parties involved.

The Staff member is successfully logged into the system. The maintenance request from the Tenant already exists in the system.

 If the reported issue is a duplicate or invalid, the Staff can change the status to "Cancelled" and input the reason.
Exceptions:
 
7a. Data Saving Failure:

Priority:
 High / Must Have
Frequency of Use:
Frequent (Every time a technical issue occurs in a room).

Only assigned Staff members or the Landlord have the authorization to change the maintenance status. A task marked as "Completed" cannot be reverted to "Pending".
Other Information:
Linked directly to the "Maintenance Request" data flow in the Context Diagram.
Assumptions:
The notification service is functional so tenants receive instant updates.

UC 20: Terminate Rental Contracts
Functional Description Template
UC ID and Name:
UC 20 - Terminate Rental Contracts
Created By:
dungda
Date Created:
03/06/2026
Primary Actor:
Landlord
Secondary Actors:
Tenant, Staff (Assists with room inspection), Bank/E-Wallet (For online deposit refunds).
Trigger:
A rental contract reaches its expiration date or early termination is requested by either party.
Description:
Handles the checkout process by recording final utility meter readings (electricity and water), calculating remaining balances (credits/debts), deducting costs for property damage (if any), and officially closing the tenant's active file. (Maps directly to feature 
FE-03
).
Preconditions:
The Tenant's rental contract status is currently "Active".
Postconditions:
The contract status changes to "Terminated". The room status resets to "Available". The tenant's active file is closed.
Normal Flow:
1.
       
Landlord selects the targeted Tenant's active contract and clicks "Terminate Contract / Checkout".
2.
       
The system loads the checkout settlement form.
3.
       
Landlord inputs the final electricity and water meter readings. The system automatically computes utility costs based on configured rates.
4.
       
Landlord inputs any property damage deduction costs with an itemized description.
5.
       
The system computes the final financial summary: Refund/Collect Amount = [Security Deposit] - [Outstanding Rent] - [Accrued Utilities] - [Damage Fees].
6.
       
Landlord reviews the final closing financial calculations and clicks "Confirm Termination".
7.
       
The system sets the contract status to "Terminated", changes the room status to "Available", closes the tenant's active file, and outputs a liquidation report in PDF format sent to the Tenant.
Alternative Flows:
4a. No Property Damage:
 Landlord leaves damage fee fields blank -> The system defaults the property damage cost to 0.
Exceptions:
3a. Illogical Meter Readings:
 The input final utility meter reading is lower than the previous billing cycle's reading -> The system generates an error message and restricts settlement.
Priority:
High / Must Have
Frequency of Use:
Medium (Triggered whenever a tenant checks out of a room).
Business Rules:
Upon closing the tenant's active file, all automated permissions and credentials assigned to the Tenant for facility access or app features are revoked.
Other Information:
The final generated PDF acts as a binding mutual legal agreement for deposit clearance.
Assumptions:
Physical room inspection has been successfully completed by Staff/Landlord and verified with the Tenant.
